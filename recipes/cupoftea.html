A Proper Cup of Tea

This is dedicated to Brioni-Leigh Mackinnon. A woman that, while I love, does not know how to make a cup of tea. This project then is dedicated to helping her achieve her greatness in tea-making. The art of tea blending is not all that complex, but I imagine her huge brain has been used primarily for the intake of tooth nonce subjects, and has not been utilized solely to make the best cup of tea possible; and so for this reason, I will let her off.

However, now she must learn how to properly make a cup of tea and it does not, in any way, include dipping toast into it. If I even suspect you are using this recipe to dip toast into, I will take it down with good conscience.

Ingredients you will need

  • A trusty mug (a cup, not your most idiotic friend)
  • A dollop of milk (about this much)
  • A sturdy metal spoon.
  • A Yorkshire tea bag
  • An electric kettle

Steps

  1. Put your Yorkshire tea bag in your trusty mug.
  2. Stick the kettle on, and pet your dog while you wait
  3. Once boiled, pour the boiling water into the cup, a little over half way
  4. Stir for a brief period before battering the absolute living shit out of the tea bag and pressing it tightly against the sides of mug to drain the flavour
  5. Leave the tea bag in whilst you add your dollop of milk, and stir again briefly.
  6. Remove tea bag and enjoy
